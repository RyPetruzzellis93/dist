Pokemon

What is Pokemon?

The wonderful world of Pokemon is quite the vast array of options and attributes. Options range from playing the card games, to grabbing a hand-held console, to running around the physical world with a smart-phone in hand. Whether it's in the actual battling itself, in the finishing of the games, or by simply doing what the slogan implies by "catching them all", Pokemon allows gamers at any level to enjoy a sense of competition.

Understanding the Battle

The typical battle sequence in the console Pokemon game is a turn-based strategy where the user, or the "Pokemon Trainer" selects at most 6 pokemon to fill their party. The mosnters themselves in the trainer's party are equipped with at most 4 "moves" that they can use a limited amount of times in order to either attack their opponent's monsters or affect their monsters' ability to fight back. Each Pokemon is given 6 stats that effect their ability to perform in battle (HP,ATK,DEF,SATK,SDEF,SPD).

Hit Points (HP) Hit Points are the pokemon's health. When a pokemon runs out of HP, it faints and must be revived in order to continue to take part in battle.

Attack (ATK) The Attack stat gives the pokemon the ability to phycially do damage against their opponent.

Defense (DEF) The Defense stat gives the pokemon the ability to phycially cut the damage against them from their opponent.

Special Attack (SATK) The Special Attack stat gives the pokemon the ability to do element/special damage against their opponent.

Special Defense (SDEF) The Special Defense stat gives the pokemon the ability to cut the element/special damage against them from their opponent.

Speed (SPD) Speed is pretty self-explanitory. It is simply how fast the pokemon is. In a turn based game, the faster pokemon goes first!
